## Act 1 — Entry and rupture (May 2019 → July 2020)

### Meeting — Apr 30 → May 1 2019, St Andrews  **[OPENING SCENE / CH 1]**

**Sequence:**
1. Evening at narrator's house (5 Windmill Road) with friends — weed, beer, music, ordinary.
2. Mikael arrives with three foundation-programme girls ("школьницы" — ironic, not literal): Lera, Anna-Maria, Sasha.
3. Party at the house. Tom (gay friend) charming. Narrator on music, listening, not engaging.
4. Migration to the girls' dorm for more drinks.
5. Migration to the seaside — East Sands, pre-dawn, May Dip location/ritual.
6. Narrator very drunk. Per Lera's later testimony, he made timid passes at her. He does not remember making them.
7. Return home. Falling asleep. First unbidden thought of her.

**Narrator's state at book open (critical):** One year post-Sergeyeva. Still in love with her. Actively grieving. Functional but hollow. His "zero interest" in the three girls is *unavailability*, not aloofness. He is not in the market. The chapter's key is grief — never named, felt as weight.

**Craft:**
- First sentence is NOT her appearing. First sentence is the ordinary evening she will interrupt.
- Three girls; only Lera ends up getting weight — but only in retrospect. Don't single her out on arrival.
- The seaside is rendered in fragments; the narrator's memory is explicitly contested (Lera told him later, he doesn't remember). This plants unreliable-narrator on page 1.
- Falling asleep: *something moved in me that hasn't moved in a year.* The grief just turned in its sleep. Shape of Sergeyeva-damage begins reshaping around Lera. Theme born on page 1, unnamed.

**Target:** ~3,000–3,500 words across 7 beats (see `04_act1_outline.md` Ch 1).

## Recurring motifs / refrains

- **"I never asked you to do this."** Her line. Appears at the drop-out fight and the Glasgow-move fight. Candidate refrain — seed early, echo through, let its meaning shift each time.
- **Bureaucratic rejection.** Home Office refuses him. Her parents block her visits. Visas. Spreadsheets. Courts. Paperwork as love and its denial.
- **Touch and the flinch.** He reaches (hand drum, squeeze, playful slap). She snaps. He keeps reaching. Both readings exist simultaneously on the page.
- **Deleted records / lost archives.** July 2020 deletion. The hiddenness (she never tells parents or friends). Unspeakable things that leave no written trace.
- **Travel as proof of love.** Tickets offered. Trains. 12-hour buses. Visa stay. Istanbul flights. £100 bouquet left behind.
- **Sacrifice as debt.** He pays into an account she never opened. When he demands the balance, she says *I never signed up*.
