## The root claim — LOCKED

> **The damage you take from someone you loved becomes the shape of love you offer next.**

One sentence. Every scene in the book is evidence for this claim, or an echo of it, or a rhyme with it. The book does not state this on the page. The narrator may not understand it. The reader arrives at it by the end.

**Corpus anchor:**
- *Сергеева уничтожила меня* — Chief Garvey, 2025-01-04 01:42
- *You destroyed my self esteem* — leeeroy, 2025-01-27

Two years apart, same construction, two different speakers. The chain is literal. That's the book in two text messages.

## How the subsidiary themes collapse into the root

### #1 (sacrifice → debt) — the *shape*
One of the specific shapes his damaged love takes. Because Sergeyeva hollowed him out, the love he offers Lera is *I will give you everything.* That's the damaged shape. The debt he accumulates, and her refusal to pay it, is that shape meeting reality. #1 is *how* the damage transmits in Lera's chapter.

### #2 (self-erasure → nothing left) — the *consequence*
Because he's trying to fill what Sergeyeva emptied, he erases himself chasing it — drops out, moves cities, deletes the archive. Every erasure is the same damaged gesture repeating.

### #3 (every home rejects you) — the *wider pattern*
Same dynamic with places. The UK rejects him bureaucratically for the same reason Lera rejects him emotionally: he keeps offering a damaged shape — over-invested, debt-accumulating, asking to be owed — and homes, like women, don't want to be owed. The UK plot is the structural rhyme.

### #4 (still in love two years later) — the *evidence*
If the book's claim is that damage transmits and endures, his still-loving-her two years after is the proof. His voice — writing from 2026 — is the voice of a man still carrying what Sergeyeva put in him, refracted through Lera.

## The moral geometry — clarified 2026-04-19

The book is not "innocent man destroyed by cold woman." It is not "broken man destroys innocent woman." It is:

> *A man who announced his damage brought it to a woman who thought she could carry it, and it hollowed her out to his shape.*

**The warning scene** (see `03_material_log.md` — "I am deeply broken, you'll have to deal with that") is the black center of this geometry. Lera pressed for the relationship; he warned her; she chose him anyway. She is then destroyed by the thing she was warned about.

This complicates the sympathy in both directions. The book cannot:
- Make him a pure victim (he warned her but still offered the damaged shape)
- Make her a pure victim (she accepted the warning)
- Make him a pure perpetrator (he was honest)
- Make her a pure perpetrator (she was loved, badly)

The book lives inside the question: *what does it mean to want someone who tells you in advance that loving them will cost you?* That is the question the reader should still be holding in the last chapter.

## Craft rules this theme enforces

1. **Never state the theme on the page.** If the narrator says "I think the damage we take becomes the shape of love we offer next," the book collapses into essay. The theme is *proved* through scenes.
2. **The narrator may not understand it.** In fact, the tension is often between what the narrator believes (*I loved purely; she was cold; Sergeyeva ruined me; none of it was my fault*) and what the book shows. The narrator's blind spot is the engine.
3. **Every scene earns the theme.** If a chapter doesn't contribute to the argument, it gets cut or rewritten. Ask of each chapter: *what does this prove?*
4. **The theme is not consoling.** The book does not end with the narrator healing. It ends with the chain visible.

## Narrator

**Starting vantage (May 2019, book open):** One year out from Sergeyeva. Still in love with her. Actively grieving. Functional but hollow. Has friends, is in St Andrews, moves through his life — but he is not *available* to new people. This is the emotional weather in which he meets Lera.

Critical to the theme: the damaged shape he will offer Lera is **already formed** before she walks through the door. Chapter 1 is not "boy meets girl, life changes." Chapter 1 is: a grieving man meets a girl his broken shape will eventually close around.
