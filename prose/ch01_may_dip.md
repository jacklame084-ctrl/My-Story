# Chapter 1 — May Dip

The living room was filled with smoke — thick enough you could barely see through it. God knows how many people had slept here, and how many still would. We refrained from calling our beloved crib a trap house, but we weren't far off. The worst part of the room was the floor. For some reason, in the UK, they love their carpeted floors — which people step on with boots dripping with mud. As a Russian, this has always been an enigma to me, a kind of ritual I've never got used to. Wearing clothes inside the house to save on energy bills — fine, I learned that the hard way. The slush from the street on the carpet was something else.

"Hey, Alex," said Andrew. "I know you've drifted away, but you've held this joint longer than Aragorn waited to reunite with Arwen."

I chuckled, passing it along. The reference was funny. I wished I were as composed as Aragorn when it came to the love of my life. In my case, she'd been more methodical. I could list what she did — I won't, not here, not with the music up. I mean look at me.

Still, that evening, I was glad to be with my friends doing fuck all. The house had held too many of our good nights to count. Nikita was on his laptop finishing a paper on economics. Andrew was drawing something on a whiteboard. Tom was rolling another joint. My job was the music. I love rock, especially from the sixties and seventies, and I'd just put on "Spirit in the Sky" to set the mood when Tom floated across the room with a fresh one.

"You good, dawg?" he said.

"No. Still thinking about her."

"After all this time? Come on, man. Plenty of fish in the sea."

"I wish I could control it. I don't know that I ever will. That's the part that scares me."

"Relax, dude. It's May Dip — the last one you'll ever have. Make the most of it, don't overthink. We're at uni. Fuck your ex."

"I'll try," I muttered. "Can't promise I'll enjoy it."

I turned to the window. The street lights were coming on, one by one down the lane. The ghost wasn't a figure of speech. She'd lived in this house. Her old room was Andrew's room now, and the bed we used to sleep in was still in it. The sofa was a choice. Andrew and Nikita didn't mind; they knew not to ask. None of it helped. I kept making it anyway.

My thinking got interrupted when Mikael came in. As always he was suave — LV scarf over a Burberry coat. He was doing his master's at St Andrews with Nikita; I'd only known him a short while but we'd become close fast. Something on his face said he was elated and a little mischievous.

He looked at us. "Guys, how's your evening? Anything meaningful planned?"

Andrew looked back through his large glasses, almost annoyed — we were all pretty baked.

I took another drag. Mikael, still in the doorway, made his announcement.

"We've got company. Школьницы. Three of them."

Tom stopped rolling.

The room got up. Nikita closed the laptop. Andrew went straight to the mirror. Tom licked the joint shut and handed it to me like a peace offering. I stayed on the sofa. I'd put Nirvana on without noticing — *Come as You Are* — and it was running now against the getting-ready, a song meant for being alone playing over a room that had stopped being alone.
